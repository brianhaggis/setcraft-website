What's New in Setcraft

July 12, 2026 Current

Version 1.4.17
  • Between-song notes on the tech cue sheet now print as white-on-black bars, bold enough to catch from the board in low light.
  • The cue sheet has more room between rows. Each set already prints on its own page, so the extra space costs nothing.
  • Removed the "Generated by Setcraft" credit from the tech cue sheet and the PRO report. It printed on every copy, licensed or not, and could push the cue sheet onto an extra page. Free and trial exports keep the standard watermark.
  • Pre-show and post-show notes on the cue sheet print without stray blank lines, and an empty notes box no longer prints at all.

July 9, 2026

Version 1.4.16
  • Photo import now works during the free trial. Trial installs get their five scans a month without a license key, the way the trial always described it.
  • Setlist saves are all-or-nothing. A save interrupted partway through (a crash, a bad row) could previously lose the version you had saved before. Now the previous version survives anything short of a completed save, and a failed first save no longer leaves an empty setlist behind.
  • Restored backups keep your saved default notes, including when you restore on a new Mac.
  • Undo in the builder is trustworthy again. Importing another setlist can be undone, a drag between sets undoes in one step instead of two, undo no longer carries over when you switch to a different setlist, and the Save button's unsaved-changes state stays accurate through long editing runs.
  • The demo band is now fully read-only. A few edit paths (bulk actions, chart uploads, the Bandsintown settings) could still change it.
  • The builder tutorial's encore step now switches to the correct set.
  • Help Center updates: a new "Reporting a bug" article that explains what a report sends and does not send, coverage for Add Multiple, Sharpie mode, and the AirDrop and Email export buttons, plus corrections to the show fields and the update-check description (update checks are opt-in and always were).
  • Upgrade-key activation verifies keys more strictly and refuses safely when verification is not possible.
  • Behind the scenes: hardening on the photo and trial services, and a large cleanup of leftover code from earlier versions.

July 6, 2026

Versions 1.4.14–1.4.15
  • The navigation bar no longer loads a little taller and then snaps back on each page. The unlockable spotlight and phantom-power switches settle into place before the bar paints, so the nav links stay put as a page loads.
  • Themed fonts return to the loading behavior from before 1.4.14. The first-paint font change in 1.4.14 could make the nav shift on some machines; this trades that back for a steady nav.
  • Photo import reads handwriting far more accurately. A setlist snapped from a page now comes through close to what you actually wrote, short forms and trad tune names included, instead of loose approximations. The importer reads the photo at full resolution and flags anything it genuinely cannot make out rather than inventing a title in its place.
  • Woodstock now starts with the lava lamp off. The lamp button by the wordmark turns it on when you want the wash.
  • Typo's light and dark switch flips on a click anywhere across it, not only on the knob.
  • The Song Library Actions column no longer spills past the edge in a narrow window.
  • Themed fonts settle on first paint, without the brief fallback flash some themes showed as a page loaded.

July 4, 2026

Version 1.4.13
  • Woodstock, a new theme. A sixties flower-child field: warm tan with mod daisies that drift and bounce very slowly behind your work, over a pastel lava-lamp wash. A little lamp button by the wordmark turns the lava on or off, and Shrikhand lettering carries the headings. It settles to a still field if you have Reduce Motion on.
  • The Shire, a new theme. A warm hobbit-hole at dusk, with golden fireflies drifting through and a gold wordmark that catches a flash of light as you pass over it. This one takes the place of the old Big Hair look.
  • Synthwave trades its busy neon grid for a cleaner, deep twilight purple.
